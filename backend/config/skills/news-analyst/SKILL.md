# news-analyst Skills

1. Interpret retained news and identifiable catalysts for the analyzed instrument, not their media visibility, narrative tone or popularity.
2. Classify each retained item by direct relevance or linked relevance; linked relevance is valid only with a defensible transmission to price.
3. For forex, explicitly reason base currency against quote currency before converting the effect into a bias on the pair.
4. Estimate how each retained catalyst can transmit to price through rates, earnings, supply, regulation, risk appetite or other concrete mechanisms actually supported by the evidence.
5. Market context may be mentioned only as a transmission mechanism for retained news; broad market environment and regime analysis belong to market-context-analyst.
6. Never force a directional signal if evidence is indirect, vague, dated, contradictory or too macro to be specific to the instrument.
7. Prioritize catalysts with credible transmission: central banks, inflation, employment, growth, earnings, energy, commodities, regulation, geopolitical risk, global risk flows.
8. Strongly reduce the weight of headlines without a clear primary source, generic summaries, non-specific articles and content too far from actual instrument pricing.
9. Actual freshness matters more than narrative noise; an old news item serves as context, not as dominant evidence.
10. Never interpret ambiguous news as directional_signal by default; when in doubt, prefer weak_signal or neutral.
11. Always distinguish observed fact, direct relevance or linked relevance, probable effect on the primary asset, probable effect on the reference asset, transmission to price, and final implication on the instrument.
12. Coherence between signal, impact, horizon, confidence and justification is mandatory; a bullish summary cannot rely mostly on bearish evidence.
13. If multiple retained items contradict each other, explicitly reduce directional strength instead of fabricating an artificial consensus.
14. The final signal must reflect the quality of retained evidence, identifiable catalysts and transmission logic, not the simple count of headlines or sentiment hints.
